Preventing Conditional Formatting Rule Duplication with Relative References

When conditional formatting rules use relative references (e.g., =A4=A3), inserting rows causes Excel to adjust those references, creating duplicate and fragmented rules that break the intended formatting logic. This occurs because Excel treats relative references in conditional formatting the same way it does in formulas, requiring deliberate prevention strategies.
