LAMBDA function fundamentals

LAMBDA creates custom named functions by defining parameters as placeholders within a formula, allowing users to reference the function by a single name rather than repeating the full formula logic. The technique simplifies formula reusability and readability by abstracting complex calculations into named, parameterized units.
